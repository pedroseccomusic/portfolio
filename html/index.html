About

Pedro Secco is a Brazilian composer and a dreamer, who listens to music with his ears, heart and brain. Having his origins in the South of Brazil, with pinhão, firewood and the most beautiful sunsets ever seen, Pedro aspires to be a musical influence that escapes the mainstream and algorithmic tendencies, proposing songs that need time, patience and calm to be heard and appreciated.
He has gratuated in Contemporary Writing and Production at Berklee College of Music, where he was exposed to different musicians, genres and lifestyles. Through thick and thin, Pedro has persevered, learning the joys and hardships of redescovering yourself time and time again.
Pedro is inpired by a miscellany of musicians and genres, such as Pat Metheny, Tom Jobim, Toninho Horta, Miles Davis, Igor Stravisnky, Maurice Ravel, Pedro Martins, Led Zeppelin, Jimi Hendrix, Gorillaz, Milton Nascimento, Lo Borges, Claude Debussy, J. S. Bach. Today, he focuses on Jazz, Brazilian Instrumental Music, MPB (Brazilian Popular Music), Choro, Rock, and Indie/Alternative.
Pedro Secco sees himself as someone who can spread art inside and out of his community. He loves soccer (or football, as it should be called), breakfast and a good sense of humor. His work is passionate, obsessive, full of perspective. He takes his time to mature concepts and sounds, transforming experience in music. Pedro walks through life with respect and optimism, always carrying his angels by his side: friends, family, mentors, colleagues, everyone that has left a mark, a story, a reflection, an opportunity.

Underscoring & Tracks

Original compositions focused on atmosphere, storytelling and audiovisual support.

Library Cues

These are underscoring projects for an advanced-level class named Writing for Library, in the Contemporary Writing and Production curriculum at Berklee College of Music. The class required you to write, record, and mix cues that carried specific tones and flavors to be hypothetically placed in TV shows.

Once Upon a Time in Boston

These are tracks written for the movie Once Upon a Time in Boston. The movie follows a chinese student that moves to Boston and comes across different realities, lifestyles and culture.
